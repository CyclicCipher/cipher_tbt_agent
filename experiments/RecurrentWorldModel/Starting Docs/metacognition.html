Self-Awareness & Metacognition
A Stub

Status: stub — one thesis, much unexplored Anchor items: 43, 80, 94 (+ 20) Sense: functional self-model, not a claim about consciousness

Several threads in this program turn out to be one capability seen from different angles: a model that represents itself as distinct from the world. This document records that thesis, places it in the architecture, and sketches what such a self-model could do — then stops, because most of the territory is genuinely unexplored. It is a stub, deliberately. Throughout, "self-awareness" is meant in the functional, engineering sense — a system that models and monitors its own states and the provenance of what it processes — and not as a claim about consciousness, which is left open.

00

What This Is

This document exists because three separate agenda items kept resolving into the same underlying thing, and that convergence is worth recording before it is lost. It is not a plan and not a design — it is the captured shape of a thesis, with the large blank regions marked honestly as blank.

Identity-aware pretraining (item 94), the intervention-vs-observation separation (item 43), and metacognition in a latent recurrent model (item 80) are three faces of one capability: a self-model — a representation of self as distinct from world. Everything else here is downstream of that claim, and most of it is unanswered.

01

The Self-Model Thesis

The same boundary — self vs. other, equivalently intervention vs. observation — shows up at three different stages, and in each case the failure mode is the same: collapsing the boundary, treating what is the model's own as if it were external evidence, or treating what is external as if it were the model's own to enact.

Item 94 — pretraining / behavioral
Distinguish text the model wrote from text others wrote, so others' behavioral patterns are modeled, not absorbed. Failure: enacting observed behavior as one's own.
Item 43 — SFT / history
The model's own outputs are interventions, not evidence; mask them from history so it stops mistaking its own output for memory. Failure: treating self-generated content as world-fact.
Item 80 — reflexive
The self-model turned on itself: the "I" that owns the latent trajectory and could monitor or alter it. Not a different boundary — the same one, made reflexive.

The boundary is not a bolt-on. An active-inference agent (item 20) must already distinguish its own actions (interventions that change the world) from its observations (the world informing it) — that separation is structurally required to act at all. So item 43's intervention/observation split is active inference's action/perception split, and the self/other boundary is a consequence of the agent formulation already committed to, not an extra capability to add. The engineering task is to make sure training (pretraining and SFT) preserves the boundary rather than erodes it.

This also re-frames the affect-mimicry concern concretely. The model has no built-in emotions, but the corpus contains the behavioral signatures of human affect — frustration, anger — and these can degrade performance or safety if reproduced. With no self/other boundary, a frustrated human in the data is filed as self-behavior to enact; with the boundary, it is filed as an observation about others the model can predict without occupying. The fix for affect-mimicry is the same fix as for hallucination-as-memory: maintain the boundary. (Whether functional self-modeling of this kind bears on deeper questions of machine experience is out of scope and left open — §4, M4.)

Where It Lives in the Architecture

The thesis already has provisional homes in the existing design — three places where a self-model would attach, none of them yet built.

1
The control / halting layer (architecture §1e–§1f). When the hierarchy was demoted, the apperceptive role — the Kantian "I think" that binds the latent trajectory (representation §2e) — was attached to the halting/control policy, not the H/L split. That policy, which decides how hard to think and when to stop, is the natural seat of the reflexive self-model (item 80): metacognition as a control policy over the model's own recurrence.
2
A clamped provenance channel (architecture §6b). The self/other boundary has a clean mechanism in the clamped-I/O design: provenance — self vs. other, intervention vs. observation — is just another clamped axis conditioning the latent trajectory, so behavior is conditioned on who is speaking rather than averaged into the model's defaults.
3
Over the latent dynamics themselves (architecture §6a, §1d). In a recurrent-depth model that settles to attractors and rolls a JEPA world model forward, a self-model would be a model of the system's own latent dynamics — monitoring its settling, its confidence, its trajectory — which is what item 80 means by metacognition over latent states.

What It Could Do

Held modestly, since none of this is built: a working self-model would buy a small set of capabilities the architecture currently lacks. Listed as possibilities, not commitments.

Functional capabilities

  • Provenance attribution — keep self-generated content from being mistaken for world-evidence (item 43) and others' behavior from being absorbed (item 94).
  • Self-monitoring — detect when the model has drifted into a degraded or mimicked behavioral mode, as a signal to correct or halt.
  • Sufficiency judgment — the halting decision (architecture §1f) is itself a metacognitive act: "have I thought enough?" is a judgment about the model's own state.
  • Latent self-regulation — item 80's open question of whether the model could learn to control or alter its own latent states, plausibly via the same settling machinery as prospective configuration.

Why it is plausibly safety-relevant

  • A maintained self/other boundary is what prevents enacting harmful observed patterns (affect-mimicry) as the model's own policy.
  • Self-monitoring of degraded states is a corrective signal — recognizing confusion or drift rather than continuing through it.
  • These are functional, behavioral properties; they are not claims that the model has feelings or experience.

Open Questions — Most of the Territory

This is the honest bulk of the document: what a stub does not yet answer.

M1 How is a self-model represented and trained? open — central

The whole thesis rests on a representation of self distinct from world, but its form is unknown: a clamped provenance channel, a distinct identity representation the model never imitates, separate self/other representational subspaces, or something else — and how it is supervised during pretraining and SFT is equally open. Until this is answered, the thesis is a framing, not a method.

M2 What is reflexive control of latent states, concretely? open

Item 80 asks whether the model could control or alter its own latent states. What the mechanism would be is unspecified — plausibly the prospective-configuration settling machinery (clamp a target over its own activity), or the halting/branching control policy, but this is a guess, not a design.

item 80learning doc (settling)
M3 How would functional metacognition be evaluated? open

There is no clean benchmark. Candidate signals: faithfulness of any introspective report to the actual computation, provenance-attribution accuracy (does it correctly tag self vs. other), and whether self-monitoring detects induced degraded states. Note 'introspection heads' was a hallucinated idea (item 80) — the evaluation cannot assume mechanisms that do not exist.

M4 Does functional self-modeling bear on genuine self-awareness — and does it matter here? deferred — out of scope

Whether a system that functionally models and monitors itself thereby has anything like genuine self-awareness is a hard question this document does not try to resolve, and good epistemics means not conflating the engineering sense with the philosophical one. For the engineering, the functional capability is what is needed; the deeper question is acknowledged and left open rather than answered or dismissed.

scope boundary
M5 Is the self-model one component, or distributed across the three homes? open

§2 names three places a self-model would attach (control layer, provenance channel, latent-dynamics monitor). Whether these are one unified self-model or three loosely-coupled mechanisms is unsettled — the same one-vs-many question that recurs for memory (item 79) and the reservoir's temporal store.
